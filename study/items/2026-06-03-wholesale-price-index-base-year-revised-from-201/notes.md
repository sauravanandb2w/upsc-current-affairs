## Summary / story

India is revising the WPI base year from 2011–12 to 2022–23 and gradually transitioning toward Producer Price Index (PPI) to modernize inflation measurement and align with global standards.

## Facts

test test **testtest**

- WPI basket expanded:  - **697 → 957 items**
- Weight structure broadly unchanged:  - Manufactured products still have highest weight (~63%).
- Government will experimentally introduce:  - Producer Price Index (PPI)
- After around **5 years**, WPI may be replaced by PPI.
- Services PPI will also be introduced for:  - banking, - telecom, - insurance, - railways etc.
- WPI is released **monthly**.
- Reserve Bank of India mainly uses CPI for inflation targeting.

## Static connection

### Inflation Indices in India

- CPI → Consumer inflation
- WPI → Wholesale inflation
- PPI → Producer inflation

### Difference between WPI & PPI

| WPI | PPI |
| --- | --- |
| Wholesale prices | Producer prices |
| Includes taxes/trade distortions | Cleaner producer cost |
| Mainly goods | Goods + services |
| Older methodology | International standard |

### Why shift to PPI?

- Better GDP deflator
- More accurate inflation measurement
- Globally accepted methodology
- Better reflects producer costs

## Exam angle

### Q1. What is the recent WPI news?

**Answer:**
India revised the WPI base year from **2011–12 to 2022–23** and will experimentally introduce PPI.

### Q2. What is WPI?

**Answer:**
Wholesale Price Index measures changes in wholesale prices in the economy.

### Q3. How frequently is WPI released?

**Answer:**
WPI is released **monthly**.

### Q4. Which organization releases WPI?

**Answer:**
Office of Economic Adviser under the Commerce Ministry.

### Q5. What is PPI?

**Answer:**
Producer Price Index measures prices received by producers for their output.

### Q6. Why is India shifting toward PPI?

**Answer:**
Because PPI:

- better reflects producer costs,
- improves GDP estimation,
- aligns with global standards,
- is more accurate than WPI.
